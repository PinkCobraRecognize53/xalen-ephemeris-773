## When You Need a Commercial License

The Apache 2.0 license is sufficient for most users — including commercial use,
redistribution, **and proprietary/closed-source modifications** (Apache 2.0 is a
permissive license, not copyleft; you are *not* required to open-source your
changes). You only need a commercial license for things Apache 2.0 does **not**
grant:

- **Attribution / NOTICE waiver** -- Apache 2.0 requires preserving copyright
  notices and the NOTICE file in distributions. A commercial license removes
  that obligation (e.g. for embedded/white-label products).
- **Warranty and indemnification** -- Apache 2.0 is provided strictly "AS IS"
  with no warranty. A commercial license adds a limited warranty and IP
  indemnification.
- **Enterprise support** -- SLA-backed response times, priority bug fixes, and
  direct access to the maintainers.
